Overview

Project Background

MSoftech's CTO and CEO both worked on Samsung SDS America projects for 3.5 years — fully remote, fully async, fully text-based. Every requirement, every review, every decision was handled through structured documentation and collaboration tools, not real-time meetings.

This experience became the foundation of MSoftech's pair collaboration model and document-centered delivery methodology. The operational practices proven at Samsung SDS America — daily deployments, ticket-driven workflows, timezone-leveraged development cycles — are now core to every MSoftech engagement.

Workflow

How We Worked

The timezone difference wasn't a limitation — it was a design element. While the U.S. team slept, MSoftech shipped. Every morning, progress was already waiting. The project moved 24 hours a day.

U.S. Business Hours
Collect & Organize Requests
The U.S. team reviews delivered results, logs change requests and feedback in JIRA tickets, and organizes priorities before end of day.
After U.S. Hours (KST Daytime)
Build & Deploy
MSoftech works through organized requests, implements changes, commits code, and deploys results to the staging environment — all before the U.S. team wakes up.
Next U.S. Morning
Review & Close
The U.S. team arrives to find completed work already deployed. No issues → close ticket. Minor fix needed → add comment. Urgent → Slack for immediate resolution.
Daily
4-Hour Overlap
U.S. morning hours
Real-time availability during U.S. morning hours for urgent questions, screen sharing, and quick decision-making. Enough overlap for synchronous communication when needed.
On Demand
8-Hour Full Coverage
Release · Incidents · Milestones
Full U.S. business hours coverage for releases, incident response, and critical milestones. The assumption that 'remote means slow response' gets disproven.
Tools

Collaboration Stack

Every decision was documented. Every request was tracked. Every change was auditable. No 'what did we agree on?' disputes — the tools enforced transparency by design.

JIRA
Ticket-driven workflow. Every request logged with requester, timestamp, screenshots. Full audit trail.
Confluence
Shared documentation. Specs, requirements, and design docs co-edited in real time. Single source of truth.
Slack
Text-based communication. Threaded discussions, file sharing, code blocks. Every conversation recorded.
Daily Reports
English development reports emailed daily. Completed items, in-progress, tomorrow's plan, and ticket status updates.
