Overview

Project Overview

Hydro Simulation Platform is a unified decision-support tool built for groundwater and hot spring development stakeholders. It consolidates the entire pre-development workflow — from cost estimation and demand calculation to investment scenario modeling — into interactive simulators that replace traditional spreadsheet-based planning.

Built on domain expertise accumulated through AI AquaLab and AI Geological Analysis, the platform extends MSoftech's water resource technology stack with 6 specialized simulation modules, a nationwide hot spring database (466 records from the Ministry of Public Administration), and an interactive geological history timeline spanning 2.5 billion years.

Simulation Modules

Six specialized simulation modules cover the entire lifecycle of groundwater and hot spring development — from initial cost estimation through demand forecasting, investment modeling, and geological education.

MODULE 01
Hot Spring Cost Simulation
End-to-end cost estimation — exploration, drilling, casing, testing, licensing — with 5-stage verification strategy and Sunk Cost risk warnings.
MODULE 02
Groundwater Cost Simulation
Exploration options (dowsing/MT survey), drilling costs by depth, 3-stage casing configuration, and fracture zone risk assessment toggle.
MODULE 03
Hot Spring Demand Simulator
KDS 31 10 15 compliant demand calculation — guest room supply (Module A), public bath operation (Module B), and expert margin adjustment (Module C).
MODULE 04
Investment Simulation
Full ROI modeling with multi-well scenarios, annual revenue projections, break-even analysis, and cumulative profit charts based on real project data.
MODULE 05
Nationwide Hot Spring Map
466 hot spring records (2025 Ministry data) with interactive map, multi-filter search (region, yield, temperature, depth, pH), and detailed info popups.
MODULE 06
Korean Peninsula Geological Timeline
2.5 billion years of geological history in 6 interactive stages — with cross-section diagrams, key formation events, and direct connections to hot spring development rationale.
